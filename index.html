MuddPi Mark IV

An educational tool for HMC Microprocessors Laboratory

Working with Professor David Money Harris, Fall 2014 to Present

The curriculum for Harvey Mudd's E155: Microprocessor Systems class is changing from MIPS to ARM. This prompted a redesign of the class's educational development board. The FPGA was replaced with a new Cyclone IV, the PIC32 is being switched out for a Raspberry Pi, among other changes to suit the new curriculum.

Kraken

A custom heavy lift quadcopter

Working with Alex Alves, Summer 2014 to present

This is a work in progress. I've always wanted to have a medium lift quadcopter. The possibilities are endless. However, It turns out that if you don't want to buy one that's entirely pre-made, there's no easy kits to get started. Alex Alves and I have researched and bought parts to construct our own such quadcopter. We're currently figuring out the control system. We hope to be able to use it semi-autonomously for such tasks as being a sock-bomber for the semesterly school-wide game of Humans vs. Zombies.

Bluetooth Window Lock Controller

An Android phone controllable window lock operator

Spring 2014

Occasionally, it's nice to be able to use my window as an entry and exit to my dorm room. However, having an unlocked window can be a large security risk. Using my phone, an Arduino Pro Mini, a Bluetooth BlueSMiRF, and some scrap parts from old projects, I created this window lock controller that I could connect to from my phone. With the power to lock and unlock my window from up to 100 m away, I get the best of freedom and security out of my dorm room window.

Web-controlled Nerf Turret

A Raspberry Pi Nerf Turret with an internet interface

Worked with Leif Park Jordan, Spring 2014

Sometimes, during long hours of work in the engineering labs, you wish you could just go to website, and command a remote semi-automatic Nerf gun to aim and fire at your friends in the Dorm Lounge. Over the course of 6 weeks, Leif and I designed such a system including a webcam with a live feed to make sure you're aiming at the right person. PHP and javascript make a simple web interface to view your targets, rotate the turret, and fire nerf darts until the clip is out of ammo.

Experimental Model Rocket Localization

Finding model rocket flight paths with various sensors

Worked with Jessica Chen, Dot Silverman, and Katherine Yang, Spring 2013

How can you localize the flight path of a model rocket? I worked with a team to test several different methods of localization using a pressure sensor, a 6-DOF IMU, and a magnetometer. I designed the electronics payload and wrote the MATLAB code to perform the signal processing on the data.
